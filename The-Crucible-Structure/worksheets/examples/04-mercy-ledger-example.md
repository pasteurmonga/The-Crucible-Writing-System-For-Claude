# Mercy Ledger Worksheet - EXAMPLE

**Story:** The Memory Forge

---

## Understanding the Mercy Ledger

Each mercy should:
- Cost the protagonist something immediate
- Seem like a poor tactical choice at the time
- Pay off unexpectedly in Beat 31 (The Repayment)
- Demonstrate character growth

---

## Mercy 1

**Movement (when it occurs):** Movement II / Chapter 4-5

**Recipient (who receives this mercy):**

A wounded cult member Kira could kill but doesn't. During an early confrontation, she comes upon a wounded cult fighter who is helpless.

**The Act (what protagonist does):**

She spares them. They participated in the attack on her village, they serve the cult leader, they would kill her if positions were reversed, and leaving them alive risks her discovery. She spares them anyway.

**Immediate Cost (what protagonist sacrifices/risks):**

Strategic risk -- the wounded enemy knows where she is and could tell the cult. Allies question her judgment. It seems like foolish mercy.

**Later Payoff (how recipient returns the mercy):**

They provide critical intelligence about the cult. The mercy haunted them. They begin to question the cult's mission. When the time comes, they find a way to get information to her that shapes her strategy at the Apex.

**Connection to Beat 31:**

At the Apex, the intelligence they provided allows Kira to anticipate the cult leader's strategy. Without this information, she would have walked into a trap.

---

## Mercy 2

**Movement (when it occurs):** Movement II-III / Chapter 5-6

**Recipient (who receives this mercy):**

A cult underling captured during a raid. Standard practice would be to extract information and eliminate the threat. Others in her constellation expect this.

**The Act (what protagonist does):**

She shows them mercy -- lets them live, tends their wounds, and eventually releases them. She treats them as a person, not a resource to be exploited.

**Immediate Cost (what protagonist sacrifices/risks):**

Criticism from allies who see it as weakness. Lady Celene particularly objects. Some keepers question her judgment. Tam worries she's being naive. She appears soft when she should be hard.

**Later Payoff (how recipient returns the mercy):**

The underling defects at a crucial time. They escape or are released, return to the cult, but are changed. At a crucial moment in the final battle, they sabotage or withhold information from the cult. Their defection creates an opening at the Apex.

**Connection to Beat 31:**

At the Apex, the defector's sabotage delays cult reinforcements, giving Kira's forces time to reach the inner sanctum. Without this mercy, the battle would have been lost before it began.

---

## Mercy 3

**Movement (when it occurs):** Movement III / Chapter 7

**Recipient (who receives this mercy):**

Lord Harren, a political rival who endangered the keepers. He attempted to betray the keepers' location to the cult leader for political advantage. Kira catches his spy network and exposes his treachery.

**The Act (what protagonist does):**

She could have him executed for treason. The evidence is clear, and his death would be justified. Instead, she chooses exile -- he is stripped of lands and titles but allowed to live.

**Immediate Cost (what protagonist sacrifices/risks):**

Strategic disadvantage in the political landscape. The rival remains alive. Lady Celene is frustrated by what she sees as weakness. Kira loses political capital by appearing soft on traitors.

**Later Payoff (how recipient returns the mercy):**

Lord Harren brings military forces to the final battle. In exile, he discovers the cult threatens everyone, including him. He recognizes what Kira is fighting for. His political connections translate to military support -- forces that could have been enemies become allies.

**Connection to Beat 31:**

At the Apex, Harren's forces hold the eastern approach against cult reinforcements. Without his mercenaries, the cult would have overwhelmed Lady Celene's forces.

---

## Mercy 4

**Movement (when it occurs):** Movement III-IV / Chapter 8-9

**Recipient (who receives this mercy):**

Captain Theron, a trusted lieutenant of the cult leader. He is a twenty-year veteran of the cult's forces, loyal from genuine belief. Kira captures him during a convoy raid.

**The Act (what protagonist does):**

She risks everything to spare him. To spare him, she puts herself in danger -- perhaps letting him go with a message instead of extracting information by force. The cult leader will know she showed mercy.

**Immediate Cost (what protagonist sacrifices/risks):**

Personal risk and strategic exposure. The cult leader knows she showed mercy to his trusted captain. It seems like the costliest mercy yet -- she has revealed her nature to the enemy.

**Later Payoff (how recipient returns the mercy):**

Captain Theron becomes the "Unexpected Agent" at the Apex. Someone from the cult leader's inner circle turns at the crucial moment. He has seen both sides now. Kira's mercy proved the cult leader's philosophy wrong. He acts against the cult at the Apex.

**Connection to Beat 31:**

At the Apex, Captain Theron hesitates at the crucial moment, allowing Kira to reach the cult leader. His wavering loyalty -- seeded by her mercy -- creates the opening she needs. Without this intervention, she would have been stopped.

## Beat 31: The Repayment (Plan)

How do the mercies combine to help protagonist in their darkest hour?

At the Apex, when all seems lost, the four mercy recipients converge:

1. **Wounded Enemy** -- provides intelligence allowing Kira to anticipate the trap
2. **Captured Underling** -- sabotages cult communications, delaying reinforcements
3. **Political Rival** -- brings military forces that hold the eastern approach
4. **Captain Theron** -- hesitates at the crucial moment, creating the opening

Together, they prove the theme: identity is choice. Each mercy recipient was given the choice to return kindness or continue on their dark path. Each chose to repay mercy with aid. The cult leader cannot comprehend this -- in their worldview, power is everything, and kindness is weakness. But the unexpected agents prove that compassion creates allies power cannot buy.

Without any one of these mercies, the Apex would have failed. Together, they create victory.

---

## Notes

- Each mercy escalates in risk (strategic -> political -> personal)
- Each mercy recipient comes from a different sphere (enemy soldier, enemy underling, political rival, enemy leadership)
- Each payoff is specific and plot-relevant, not just thematic
- The mercies are distributed across Movements II-IV so they feel organic
- All payoffs converge in Chapter 10 at the Apex

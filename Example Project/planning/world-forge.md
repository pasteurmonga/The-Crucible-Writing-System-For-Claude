# World Forge: The Memory Forge

## The World Wound

**What Cosmic Problem Does the Quest Address:** The collective memory of the world is being erased/corrupted.

This is not just about the memory-keepers. The entire world is losing its memory:
- History is fading
- People forget ancestors, traditions, truths
- The fabric of shared understanding is fraying
- The cult accelerates this natural decay

The keepers are the last line of defense -- but even they are failing.

---

## The Power System

### Source
**Where Power Comes From:** Innate magical bloodlines -- some are born with it.

Memory-forging is not learned but inherited. Those with the gift:
- Come from specific bloodlines
- Cannot choose to have the power
- Discover it, often traumatically
- Are very rare (perhaps one per generation, or fewer)

### The Gift
**What Memory-Forgers Can Do:**
- Forge weapons from stolen memories
- Take memories from others (with touch, or through their weapons)
- Store memories in forged objects
- Access and experience stolen memories

### Limitations
**What Creates Tension:** Physical toll -- pain, exhaustion, transformation.

Each use of the power:
- Causes physical changes to the wielder
- Brings them closer to transformation
- Exhausts them proportional to the power used
- Cannot be undone once begun

### Progression
The more power used, the faster the transformation:
- Early: subtle changes (eye color, skin texture)
- Middle: visible changes (clearly inhuman features)
- Late: nearly complete transformation
- End: either mastery/reversal or full transformation into something other

---

## The History

### Previous Wielders
**What Happened to Them:** All succumbed to corruption/transformation.

Every memory-forger before the protagonist:
- Used the power
- Was changed by it
- Eventually lost themselves
- Became hunters (like the cult leader) or died resisting

The mentor may be the only exception -- and even they carry marks of the struggle.

### The Ancient Conflict
**How History Affects the Present:** An ancient war/conflict between memory-forgers still echoes.

Long ago, memory-forgers fought:
- Perhaps over the right to keep or erase memories
- Perhaps over control of the old ways
- The cult is descended from one faction
- The keepers preserve knowledge from the other

This war shaped the world and is now reaching its end.

## Key Locations

### The Village Forge (Starting Location)
**What It Is:** The protagonist's ordinary world.

- A small blacksmith's forge in a village
- Where she learned to shape metal
- Destroyed in the attack
- Symbolizes her old identity

### The Keepers' Sanctuary (Haven)
**What It Is:** The hidden place where memory-keepers gather.

- Secret and protected
- Contains accumulated memories and knowledge
- Where she learns about her power
- May be compromised during the story

### The Cult's Stronghold (Dark Heart)
**What It Is:** The seat of the cult leader's power.

- A place of erased memory
- Feels wrong (empty, silent, incomplete)
- Where stolen memories are hoarded or destroyed
- The location of the Apex confrontation

## The World Mirrors Her

**How the World Reflects the Protagonist's State:** Memories in the world respond to her -- strengthening or fading with her power.

As she grows stronger:
- Memories around her stabilize
- She can sense the history of places
- Old things respond to her presence

As she loses herself:
- Memories fade near her
- Places feel emptier
- She becomes like the cult -- an eraser of memory

At the surrender:
- The world's memory stabilizes
- No more memory-forgers means no more hunters
- The natural decay continues, but the active destruction ends

## Culture and Society

### Primary Culture
**Scope:** One primary culture (focused, intimate story).

This is not a story of clashing civilizations but of one culture's struggle:
- The village, the keepers, the cult all share cultural roots
- They are fighting over the soul of one people
- The differences are philosophical, not ethnic
- The stakes are identity, not territory

### Social Structure
The world includes:
- Common folk (villagers, farmers, artisans)
- Nobility (the political ally's sphere)
- Memory-keepers (respected but hidden)
- The cult (feared, spreading)

### Attitudes Toward Memory-Forgers
- Feared (they can take your self)
- Mythologized (old stories of their power)
- Hidden (those with the gift conceal it)
- Hunted (the cult seeks them out)

## Sensory Details

### How Memory-Forging Looks/Sounds/Feels
- Visual: forge-fire glow, metallic shimmer in the air
- Sound: hammer-strikes, crystalline ringing
- Feel: heat, weight of memories, the pull of taking

### How Transformation Manifests
- Eyes: metallic, reflective, inhuman color
- Skin: harder, with metallic sheen
- Movement: more fluid, less human
- Presence: feels wrong, like a gap in memory

### How the Cult Is Recognized
- Signs: particular symbols, clothing, speech patterns
- Effect: emptiness around them, faded memories
- Weapons: distinctive memory-forged blades
- Attitude: certain, righteous, cold
